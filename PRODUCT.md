# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is someone who wants to turn a meaningful photo and a few informal words into a small, personal digital gift they can send immediately. They are often creating on a phone, shortly before a birthday, wedding, anniversary, thank-you, or spontaneous celebration.

The secondary audience is developers and AI-agent users who want an open, reusable way to generate standalone holographic cards without depending on the managed service.

## Product Purpose

Holocard turns one photo and one prompt into a polished, interactive holographic card with a durable, shareable URL. Success means a first-time visitor can create, preview, publish, and share a card in minutes without learning design tools.

The open-source core provides deterministic templates, a standalone HTML renderer, examples, and an agent skill. The managed Cloudflare application adds hosted uploads, text refinement, revisions, storage, share metadata, and an MCP control plane.

## Positioning

Holocard makes a personal photo feel like a living collectible—not another generic e-card and not an AI-generated webpage.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: create a card.
- Secondary CTA: open a finished example.
- The line a visitor should remember after 10 seconds: “Uma foto. Um brilho. Um presente que abre no navegador.”
- Belief ladder: the result is visibly special; creating it is simple; the message remains personal; the link is easy to share; the creator remains in control.
- Proof on hand: working interactive example cards, the live creator, and transparent open-source templates. Do not fabricate testimonials or usage counts.

## Brand Personality

Tactile, celebratory, and heartfelt. The voice is warm and direct in Brazilian Portuguese, with enough playfulness to feel like opening a small gift. It should never sound like a prompt tool, a design platform, or a technical demo.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI/SaaS landing-page aesthetics: beige dashboards, gradient text, floating glass cards, empty “AI magic” claims, repeated icon grids, and developer-tool language on the consumer journey. The visual system may reference collectible cards as a physical interaction, but must not imitate Pokémon branding, frames, names, or artwork.

## Design Principles

1. Show the gift before explaining the technology.
2. Keep the person’s photo and words—not the model—at the emotional center.
3. Make the creation flow feel like assembling a keepsake, not filling a form.
4. Use motion to reveal material and light; preserve a complete still experience.
5. Keep the generated artifact deterministic, portable, and safe.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard operation, visible focus states, readable contrast, semantic controls, and a complete reduced-motion mode. Do not require pointer tilt to understand or enjoy a card. User text must remain text, not be baked only into images.
